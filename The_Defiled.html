Defiled Lore

If you are looking for the page on the Defiled Wastelands, click here.

The Defiled as a whole is an alien entity the size of the world. It has adapted to its own way of life that further alienates it from everything else in the world. The Defiled entity can be best described as a collective conscious of hosts who warped to its will, sacrificing their autonomy for endless survival. The caveats go further when the individual details are explained further.

Breakdown

Compositional

Black Bile is a waste by-product generated when Defiled matter (what the chunks are made of) and energy are created by Defiled Fissures. Throughout the game black bile undergoes a rapid chemical property change after defeating the Wall of Flesh as the entity grows more worried about the Terrarian. It shifts from being a black heavy-gas that absorbs all light to a mulberry-colored semi-solid, which only happens because black bile desublimates from it getting colder in the Wastelands. Black Bile in either state acts as a filler material for defiled objects and means of assimilating new hosts. Just touching black bile significantly increases the chances of cancer in beings and influences their likelihood of being depressed, making it harder for them to resist the Defiled Will.

Defiled chunks or simply chunks are pieces that make up all Defiled content which have a purpose beyond just being a structural component for Defiled objects. Being made out of a compound created by the entity itself, they also magically store information in the form of memories of the host they were once a part of. They are also inherently conductive to electricity and magnetic so the Defiled can operate and control their constructs properly. Chunks are regularly transfered across different Defiled objects as part of its means of survival (transfering energy, rebuilding, etc.), which often results in the loss of memories that once belonged to that host. This can also be seen as a way for the Defiled to automate the communication process and enable the entity to learn incredibly fast as experiences are shared. The Tale of Arabel talks about her rare experience about being half-assimilated to the Defiled and how she is on a quest to recover lost memories of her son who passed.

Defiled mana is a form of mana the Defiled uses to perform its magic. It is used to generate Defiled matter creating black bile, Defiled matter and energy in the form of electricity store in the chunks. Defiled mana can only be generated when mana is stolen from hosts and is converted. When a host is in the process of being assimilated by the Defiled, it will have its mana converted into Defiled mana.

Strange String, as implied by its tooltip, is the nervous system of the original host or a surrogate host. The nervous system, including the host's brain, is normally kept in-tact by the Defiled as it is used for communication and control over them. Since the nervous system relies on electrical signals received by incoming chunks or a direct nerve impulse, and when a host is doused with high amounts of electricity it essentially fries their nerves; often making them unable to respond. Some host's nervous systems are instead used to build the Defiled's neural infrastructure such as in the underground neural network on the monoliths it builds to broadcast nerve impulses.

Other

The Defiled Will is a doctrine manifested by the Defiled entity it uses to assimilate and convince hosts of its purpose. The Defiled Will is essentially a promise to a competent thinker that they can ensure their endless existence with the sacrifice of their autonomy, failing to mention many more of its caveats.

History

The Defiled entity originally arrived to the world of Terraria from a "dimensional rift" with no recollection of anything, relying solely on its instincts to survive. It began to consume things and the world itself, spreading at a fast rate before it began to cause problems for the ancient settlers of the world and the Order of Dryads. A long-lasting war known as the "Defiled War" (which is how it got its name) was waged on the Defiled where it held out against weapons and magic alike, rapidly evolving and learning more about its adversaries and how to compose its chunks. It won the war even with several of its most primitive creations still standing, which can be found in-game as the Ancient Defiled Cyclops and Ancient Defiled Phantom. However, it was tricked by the Lost Crone who forsaw its arrival and promised it "endless odds", but sacrificed herself by sending damning herself to the Dusk with it. It eventually consumed her and used her as a means of lifting its punishment to which it failed, and spent a long-time developing a train of thought with no immediate threat to its survival being present.

Long after the events of the Dark Age, an imbalance in the Terraria world prompted it to be relieved from its purgatory in the Dusk as it was given the rite of passage as a surrogate evil in the world. It woke from its dormant state and began to spread across the world, realizing that all of the resistance it had before was now gone. After realizing this, it started to slow down and reach a period of minimal progress until the Terrarian was summoned by the world.

In-game events

The Defiled has two possible timelines depending on what the player decides to do. Both of these are separately explained below.

Hero Story

The Terrarian battles through monsters and the Defiled rising to power. They work with the Dryad to cleanse the Defiled, but with progress being minimal until the Defiled Hearts are destroyed. Despite the Defiled's best efforts to deny the Terrarian, all of its hearts get destroyed and it begins to wither.

Mimic Route

Instead of heeding the Dryad's warnings, the Terrarian instead decides to keep the entity alive. The Terraria world responds by nearly killing the Terrarian off for good until the Defiled saves them. The Defiled, instead of stripping away their autonomy and warping them like everything else, instead merges with the Terrarian to grant them many powerful abilities, control over all of the Defiled and the ability to respawn again.

Trivia

- The "dimensional rift" the Defiled comes from is a direct reference to its origin from the Startropic Adventures game. In Startropic Adventures, the Defiled is solely known as "Hauai" and maintains their likeness from that game.
